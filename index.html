Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Check the helmet’s manufacture date (usually found inside) and replace it if it's over 5 years old. Also, inspect for any visible damage, cracks, or if it has been involved in a crash.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Before riding, inspect your bike for rust, tire condition, brake functionality, and any fluid leaks. Also, check that all bolts are tight and lubricate the chain to ensure it's safe to ride.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

When riding your bike, follow these key rules: Wear a helmet for safety. Obey traffic signals and signs like a car would. Ride in the direction of traffic and use bike lanes when available. Use hand signals when turning or stopping. Stay visible by wearing bright clothing and using lights at night. Yield to pedestrians and be cautious at intersections.
